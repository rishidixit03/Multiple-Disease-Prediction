DIABETES

About

Diabetes is a disease that occurs when your blood glucose, also called blood sugar, is too high.
Blood glucose is your main source of energy and comes from the food you eat.
Insulin, a hormone made by the pancreas, helps glucose from food get into your cells to be used for energy.
Sometimes your body doesn’t make enough—or any—insulin or doesn’t use insulin well. Glucose then stays in your blood and doesn’t reach your cells.


Symptoms

  • Urinating often
  • Feeling very thirsty.
  • Extreme fatigue.
  • Blurry vision.

Prerequisite

A person must have his/her report handy to fill the values needed in predicting the disease.
Following Reports/tests are needed :
OGTT test - Oral Glucose Tolerance Test will provide the value of Glucose
Blood test - will provide the value for Insulin
Skin fold test - will provide the value for Skin thickness
Blood pressure test - will provide the value of Blood pressure

Factors affecting Diabetes

* Pregnancies: Number of times pregnant.
Reports say that more number of pregnancies after the age of 35 years increase the chances of diabetes.

* Glucose: Plasma glucose concentration a 2 hours in an oral glucose tolerance test
For an OGTT :
less than 140 mg/dL normal.
140 to 199 mg/dL = at risk(indicates prediabetes).
200 mg/dL or above = high(indicates diabetes).

* BloodPressure : Diastolic blood pressure (mm Hg) is considered here. Ideal blood pressure is considered to be between 90/60mmHg and 120/80mmHg.
General Blood pressure(diastolic) range :
Below 60 = Low
60-90 = Normal
Above 90 = High

* SkinThickness: Triceps skin fold thickness (mm)

* Insulin: 2-Hour serum insulin (mu U/ml) .

* BMI: Body mass index (weight in kg/(height in m)^2) Body mass index is a value derived from the mass and height of a person.

General range for BMI is :

18.5 - 24.9 = normal/Healthy
25.0 - 29.9 = overweight
Above 30.0 = obese.

* DiabetesPedigreeFunction: Diabetes pedigree function (a function which scores likelihood of diabetes based on family history)
Higher Pedigree Function means patient is more likely to have diabetes.

* Age: Age of a person in years.
